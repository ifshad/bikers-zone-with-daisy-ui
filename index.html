Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Check for visible damage like cracks or deformities. Examine the foam for deterioration, and ensure straps are intact and secure. Discoloration, worn-out padding, and outdated safety standards are signs of an aging helmet. If it's over 3-5 years old, or has been in an accident, replace it. Prioritize your safety; invest in a new helmet if there are any doubts about its condition.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that has been in storage, perform a safety check. Check the tires for cracks and proper inflation. Inspect the brakes for functionality, and ensure they engage smoothly. Examine the chain for rust and lubricate it if necessary. Verify that the gears shift correctly. Inspect the frame for any signs of damage or rust. Test the handlebars, pedals, and seat for stability. Once you've done a thorough inspection and made any necessary repairs, it should be safe to ride. However, if you're unsure about the bike's condition, have it checked by a professional bike mechanic before riding.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, follow these essential rules for a safe and enjoyable experience: 1. Always wear a properly fitting helmet. 2. Obey traffic laws, signals, and signs. 3. Ride in the same direction as traffic. 4. Use hand signals to indicate turns and stops. 5. Stay visible by wearing bright clothing and using lights at night. 6. Yield to pedestrians and other vehicles when required. 7. Maintain a safe distance from parked cars to avoid accidents. 8. Stay alert and aware of your surroundings. 9. Avoid distractions like using your phone while riding. 10. Regularly inspect and maintain your bike for optimal performance and safety. Adhering to these rules promotes safety and helps you become a responsible cyclist.
